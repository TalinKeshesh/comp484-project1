Origins & Formation

Linkin Park formed in 1996 in Agoura Hills, California. The early group started under the name Xero, building a style that mixed rock, hip-hop, and electronic elements. In the late 1990s they went through major changes, including a lead vocalist switch, and began shaping the sound that would later define their debut era.

Key points to include

  • Early name: The band first worked as Xero (1996–1999) before changing names.
  • Early lineup: Key members included Mike Shinoda, Brad Delson, Rob Bourdon, Joe Hahn, and Dave “Phoenix” Farrell (early).
  • Vocalist change: After early vocalist Mark Wakefield left, Chester Bennington joined in 1999.
  • “Hybrid Theory” era name: The group used the name Hybrid Theory before settling on Linkin Park.
  • First releases: Their Hybrid Theory EP originally released in 1999 (under the “Hybrid Theory” name) helped build early momentum.

Mini timeline

  • 1996: The band forms in Agoura Hills, California, and begins developing music as Xero.
  • 1996–1999: Xero era — early demos, experimentation, and refining their sound and roles.
  • 1998: Early vocalist Mark Wakefield departs (lineup shifts).
  • 1999: Chester Bennington joins; the band moves toward the Hybrid Theory era and releases the Hybrid Theory EP.
  • 2000: The group finalizes the Linkin Park identity and heads into the debut album era (released later in 2000).

Band Members
